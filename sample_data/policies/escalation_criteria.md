# Policy: Escalation Criteria and Procedures
## Document ID: POL-OPS-001
## Version: 5.0 | Effective: 2024-01-01
## Category: Operations

### 1. Escalation Levels
#### Level 1: Senior Analyst
- Cases exceeding $5,000
- Low confidence recommendations (< 0.70)
- Conflicting policy applicability
- Cardholder with VIP/premium status

#### Level 2: Supervisor
- Cases exceeding $25,000
- Regulatory or compliance implications
- Repeated escalations on same account
- Merchant dispute with legal involvement
- System recommendation overridden 3+ times

#### Level 3: Operations Manager
- Cases exceeding $100,000
- Potential fraud ring indicators
- Media or reputational risk
- Cross-border regulatory issues

### 2. Escalation Response Times
| Level | Initial Response | Resolution Target |
|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | 4 hours | 2 business days |
| Level 2 | 2 hours | 1 business day |
| Level 3 | 1 hour | Same business day |

### 3. Required Information for Escalation
All escalations must include:
- Case summary and timeline
- All evidence gathered (tool outputs, policy citations)
- System recommendation and confidence score
- Reason for escalation
- Recommended next steps

### 4. De-escalation
Cases may be de-escalated when:
- Additional evidence resolves ambiguity
- Amount correction brings case below threshold
- Cardholder withdraws dispute
- Merchant provides resolution
